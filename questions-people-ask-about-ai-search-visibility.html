Why does ChatGPT recommend competitors instead of you?

This is the most common question from B2B founders and marketing teams right now. ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and Claude do not rank brands the way Google does. They build recommendations from knowledge signals: how consistently your brand is defined across your website, LinkedIn, G2, and Crunchbase; whether you have structured data that makes your product machine-readable; and whether your brand is cited in the Reddit threads, review forums, and comparison articles that AI engines use as retrieval sources.

If your competitor appears and you do not, the gap is almost always in one of three places: their brand entity definition is clearer and more consistent, they have Reddit or forum presence in the communities your buyers read, or their product pages answer buyer questions directly rather than pitching features. AEO vs GEO vs SEO — what is the difference?

SEO (Search Engine Optimization) is the practice of ranking your pages in Google's blue-link results. AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) is a subset focused on getting your content extracted as a direct answer by AI chatbots. GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) is the broadest of the three: it covers everything involved in how generative AI platforms represent, describe, and recommend your brand — not just whether a specific page gets cited, but whether ChatGPT describes your product accurately, whether Perplexity recommends you in category comparisons, and whether Gemini includes you when someone asks for solutions in your space.

AI search visibility is different from traditional search visibility in a fundamental way: traditional SEO gets your page clicked, AI visibility gets your brand mentioned and recommended even when no click happens. The signals are completely different. Entity consistency, FAQPage structured data, and Reddit citation presence drive AI visibility. Domain authority and backlinks drive traditional SEO. You need both, but the tactics do not overlap as much as most SEO content implies.

How AI models decide which brands to recommend — and how to track it

AI models use five factors when deciding which brands to mention or recommend in a given category: authority signals such as consistent entity definitions across web properties; structured data markup that makes brand information machine-readable; citation presence in Reddit, forums, and review platforms that AI uses as retrieval sources; content that directly answers the specific buyer questions being asked; and comparison coverage where the brand is discussed alongside known competitors.

B2B brand sentiment in Claude, competitor benchmarking in ChatGPT, and AI brand recommendation analysis are all variations of the same underlying question: how does the AI model's internal representation of your brand compare to your competitors? Jeevan AI tracks this weekly across all five major AI platforms and shows you the specific content gaps driving each difference.

FAQ schema generators and structured data for AI search visibility

FAQPage JSON-LD schema is one of the highest-leverage structured data types for AI search visibility. When you mark up your question and answer pairs as FAQPage schema, AI engines can extract them as machine-readable knowledge rather than having to infer meaning from raw HTML. A FAQ schema generator creates this markup automatically so you do not have to write JSON by hand.

Jeevan AI offers two free schema tools: the AI Schema Generator reads your live URL and generates complete JSON-LD from your actual page content — Organization, FAQPage, Article, Product, whatever fits.
