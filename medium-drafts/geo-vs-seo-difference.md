      A local SEO thread asked a question that got 28 replies and no satisfying answer: *&ldquo;SEO vs AI visibility , where should I focus with a limited budget? Is AI visibility a real strategy yet, or mostly a marketing buzzword?&rdquo;*

      That is the right question. Here is the honest version.

    

    
      ## They are different systems, not competing versions of the same thing

      **SEO answers the question:** Can my page rank in Google?

      **GEO answers the question:** Can AI synthesise my brand into a recommendation?

      These require different content, different structures, and different distribution strategies. A page that ranks number one on Google can still be completely absent from ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity answers.

      [BusySeed&rsquo;s research](https://www.busyseed.com/how-prompt-tracking-helps-you-navigate-algorithm-volatility) documented this clearly: pages in the top organic positions saw a 58% lower CTR when AI Overviews appeared above them. You can hold the number one Google ranking and still lose the buyer to an AI-generated answer that does not include you.

      This is not a hypothetical future risk. [Similarweb estimates AI platforms generated 1.13 billion referral visits in June 2025](https://www.similarweb.com/blog/insights/ai-news/ai-referral-traffic-winners/) , up 357% year-over-year. The buyers who are using AI for research are already doing so.

    

    
      ## What SEO does that GEO cannot replace

      SEO is still essential and will remain so. Here is what it specifically does:

      **Drives high-volume discovery traffic.** Google still handles approximately 191 billion referrals per month. AI platforms combined handled 1.13 billion. Google is still 169 times larger as a traffic source. SEO captures the majority of discovery traffic.

      **Feeds AI training data.** Content that ranks well on Google gets indexed more frequently, cited by more authoritative sources, and accumulates backlinks , all of which increase the probability of being included in AI training data and real-time retrieval. Good SEO creates the foundation that GEO builds on.

      **Works at scale for informational queries.** How-to content, educational articles, and informational guides all perform better in traditional search than in AI recommendations. AI tends to synthesise these into its own answer rather than citing a source. Google still sends traffic to the page.

    

    
      ## What GEO does that SEO cannot

      **Decision-stage queries.** &ldquo;Best [product] for [specific use case]&rdquo; queries are increasingly answered by AI with direct recommendations. [Semrush data analysed over 10 million keywords](https://www.semrush.com/blog/semrush-ai-overviews-study/) and found commercial and transactional intent query coverage in AI Overviews growing significantly. When AI answers them, the buyer often does not click through to Google results.

      **Comparison and validation queries.** &ldquo;Is [Brand] worth it?&rdquo; and &ldquo;[Brand A] vs [Brand B]&rdquo; queries are now commonly answered by AI. The brands that appear are not necessarily the ones ranking highest on Google , they are the ones with the most structured [comparison and social proof content](https://jeevanai.co.in/blog/content-gap-ai-search-visibility.html).

      **Trust-based queries.** &ldquo;Which [category] brand is most trusted?&rdquo; , AI answers these based on citable social proof signals, review platform presence, and community discussion. [Reddit presence and review volume](https://jeevanai.co.in/blog/social-proof-ai-recommendations.html) matter much more here than Google rankings.

    

    
      ## The compound effect: why both disciplines work together

      Content that ranks on Google gets indexed more authoritatively, which increases citation probability in AI answers. AI citations drive branded search volume, which signals authority back to Google. The two systems feed each other.

      The strategic question is not &ldquo;SEO or GEO?&rdquo; It is &ldquo;what do I do first?&rdquo; And the [order of operations matters significantly](https://jeevanai.co.in/blog/geo-order-of-operations.html).

    

    
      ## The order of operations: for a brand with limited budget and time

      
        
          1

          
            **Month 1: Fix the foundation , make your site legible**
            Before anything else, your website needs to be able to answer basic questions clearly: what do you do, who is it for, what problem does it solve, what proof exists. An analysis of why SaaS brands are invisible in AI search identified the pattern: &ldquo;a beautiful homepage, vague feature blurbs, no comparison pages, no use-case pages, no proof.&rdquo; Fix this before investing in either SEO or GEO.

          

        

        
          2

          
            **Month 1-2: Publish your highest-priority GEO content first**
            Three pages have the highest AI citation impact and lowest time investment: a comparison page (you vs top competitor), a use-case specific page (best [product] for [specific scenario]), and a social proof page (customer count, outcomes, testimonials). These pages are also good SEO content, so the investment serves both purposes.

          

        

        
          3

          
            **Month 2-3: Build your Google authority baseline**
            Create a consistent publishing schedule for informational content targeting keywords your buyers search. This feeds the Google ranking -> AI training data compound loop. Two quality posts per month is better than twelve low-quality posts.

          

        

        
          4

          
            **Month 3+: Measure AI visibility and close specific gaps**
            After your foundational content is live, [run an AI visibility scan](https://jeevanai.co.in/blog/how-to-audit-ai-search-visibility.html) to measure which buying factors are still underscored. This tells you exactly what to publish next rather than guessing. Use an [AI visibility tool](https://jeevanai.co.in/ai-visibility-tool.html) to track progress across all five platforms.

      ## Frequently Asked Questions

      
        Is GEO just rebranded SEO?

        No. [GEO](https://jeevanai.co.in/glossary/generative-engine-optimisation.html) optimises for a fundamentally different system. SEO targets a crawl-and-rank model where the reward is a link in a search result list. GEO targets a synthesise-and-cite model where the reward is inclusion in an AI-generated answer. The content structures, distribution strategies, and measurement systems differ materially.

      

      
        Will GEO replace SEO?

        Not for the foreseeable future. Google still drives 169 times more traffic than all AI platforms combined. SEO remains essential for volume. GEO is the additional layer that captures decision-stage buyers who now use AI for recommendations before they click anything. Read more: [AI Search Visibility vs SEO](https://jeevanai.co.in/blog/ai-search-visibility-vs-seo.html).

      

      
        Can you rank in GEO without ranking in SEO?

        Yes, but it is harder. AI models give higher citation weight to content on high-authority domains, which correlates with SEO performance. However, Reddit threads and G2 reviews , both strong GEO signals , have nothing to do with your site&rsquo;s SEO. Community presence and review platform presence are GEO-specific investments.

      

      
        How do I know if GEO is worth investing in for my category?

        Run 5-10 queries that your buyers would ask when evaluating your category on ChatGPT or Perplexity. If your competitors appear and you do not, GEO is costing you buyers today. If neither you nor competitors appear, the category is an early-mover opportunity.

      

      
        What is the fastest GEO content to publish?

        A comparison page (&ldquo;Brand A vs Brand B&rdquo;) and a use-case specific page (&ldquo;best [product] for [specific scenario]&rdquo;). These directly address the query types where AI models most often make recommendations and produce the fastest citation movement.

      

    

    

    
      GEO and SEO are not rivals. They are two measurement systems for two different buyer journey moments , discovery (SEO) and decision (GEO). Both matter. Both compound. The worst thing to do is ignore one completely. The second-worst is to be paralysed trying to decide between them.

      For a brand with limited budget, the order of operations is: fix site legibility first, publish GEO-priority content second, build SEO authority third, measure gaps and close them fourth.
