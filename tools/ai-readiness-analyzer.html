    How It Works

    You paste a URL, and the analyzer fetches that live page and evaluates it against the factors that determine AI search visibility, not traditional ranking factors. It checks whether your brand entity is unmistakable, whether your content is structured into self-contained chunks an AI can lift, whether you have relevant schema, whether you answer the questions buyers actually ask, and whether your claims are specific enough to quote. You get an overall score and, for each factor, what the page does today and exactly what to change.

    This is the diagnostic version of the principles in our guides on building a brand entity page, the content formats AI cites, and becoming a source AI wants to cite. The analyzer scores one page; those guides explain how to fix what it finds.

    What to Do With Your Score

    Start with the lowest-scoring factor and the top fixes list. Most pages gain the most from two changes: tightening the entity definition in the opening (so the AI knows exactly what the page is and who it serves) and restructuring content into answer-led, self-contained sections. Add or correct schema with our schema markup generator, then re-run the analyzer to confirm the score moved.

    The analyzer scores how citable a page is. Whether AI engines are actually citing your brand right now, and how that changes over time, is what the Jeevan AI dashboard tracks across every major engine.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    What does the AI-Readiness Page Analyzer check?

    It fetches your live page and scores it on six factors: entity clarity, extractable structure, schema markup, FAQ coverage, answer-led content, and specificity and evidence. It returns an overall score plus a finding and a concrete fix for each factor.

    Is it free?

    Yes, free with no signup. Paste a public URL and get a scorecard with specific fixes. The page must be publicly accessible (not behind a login) for the tool to read it. For ongoing tracking of whether AI engines cite your brand over time, Jeevan AI offers a free scan and paid monitoring.

    How is this different from a traditional SEO audit?

    An SEO audit checks ranking factors (titles, meta, speed, backlinks). This checks AI-citation factors: extractability, entity clarity, structured data, and quotable specificity. They overlap on fundamentals, but AI readiness weights extractability and entity clarity far more heavily.
