What AI Brand Perception Tracking Actually Does

An AI brand perception tracker runs a fixed set of prompts against AI engines on a schedule, then records which brands get mentioned, how often, and in what tone. It turns a one-off manual check into a measurable trend line.

The core mechanic is simple. You define a list of prompts that match how buyers in your category actually ask, for example "best CRM for a small Indian startup" or "is [your brand] good for enterprise security." The tool sends those prompts to engines like ChatGPT and Perplexity, captures the responses, and parses them for brand mentions, sentiment, ranking order, and the sources cited.

That last part matters. Perplexity in particular shows its sources on every answer, so a good tracker also tells you which pages the AI is leaning on to describe you. What ₹5,000 Will Not Buy

Setting expectations matters, because the temptation is to assume the cheap tier does everything the expensive one does. It does not.

Enterprise platforms sit well above this budget. Comparisons of Profound, Peec, and Otterly show Profound starting around 99 dollars and climbing to 499 dollars or more, with Peec AI around 89 to 99 dollars. What you pay extra for at that level is depth: far more tracked prompts, conversation-level analytics, more engines, agency and multi-client features, and richer reporting. There is also a common trap in the mid-tier, where add-ons quietly inflate the bill. Otterly, for instance, charges extra for Gemini and Google AI Mode coverage on lower plans, so the headline price is not always the real price.

For a single brand that mainly wants to answer one question, namely how ChatGPT and Perplexity describe it and whether that is improving, the sub-₹5,000 tier is genuinely enough to start. You can graduate later if you need agency-grade depth.

How to Choose the Right One

  1. Check engine coverage, not just the logo wall. Confirm ChatGPT and Perplexity are on the base plan, and whether Gemini and Google AI Mode cost extra.
  2. Count the prompts. A plan with 15 prompts is fine for one brand and a few core queries. If you track many product lines, you will outgrow it quickly.
  3. Look for sources, not just mentions. The tools that show which pages the AI cites are far more actionable, because they tell you what to fix.
  4. Confirm the billing currency. Most charge in dollars or euros. Factor in the exchange rate and any card forex fees from your Indian bank.
  5. Prefer a free starting point. Run a free scan or audit first, so you know the tool surfaces something useful before you commit a monthly fee.
