AEO vs GEO: What Is the Difference, and Which Should You Focus On?

The two acronyms get used interchangeably, and the confusion is everywhere in marketing discussions. They are related but not identical. Here is the clear distinction, and the practical answer to which one your B2B team should actually pursue.

This guide defines Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) and Generative Engine Optimization (GEO), explains exactly how they differ and overlap, and gives a practical recommendation on which to prioritize for B2B brands in 2026. Short version: pursue GEO as the strategy, use AEO as a technique within it.

Spend ten minutes in any marketing community and you will see AEO and GEO used as if they mean the same thing. Marketers ask "what is AEO strategy," "what is the benefit of AEO," and "should I do AEO or GEO" in the same breath, often without a clear sense of how the two actually differ. The confusion is understandable, because they overlap heavily. But they are not the same, and knowing the difference changes how you allocate effort.

What Is AEO (Answer Engine Optimization)?

Answer Engine Optimization is the practice of structuring content so it can be extracted and served as the direct answer to a specific question. It predates the LLM era. AEO is what powered featured snippets, Google's answer boxes, voice assistant responses, and the "People Also Ask" boxes. The core AEO technique is writing clear, self-contained answers to specific questions, often supported by FAQ schema, so that an answer engine can lift your text and present it as the response.

AEO is fundamentally question-and-answer centric. Its unit of work is the individual question: identify a question your audience asks, then make your content the cleanest, most extractable answer to it.

What Is GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)?

Generative Engine Optimization is the broader practice of optimizing your brand and content to be cited and recommended within the generated responses of large language models like ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, and Gemini. GEO is brand-and-entity centric. Its unit of work is not a single question, but your brand's overall presence: whether AI models understand what your brand is, trust it enough to recommend it, and have enough corroborated information to cite it across a whole range of buyer queries.

GEO includes answering questions well, but it goes further into entity authority, multi-source corroboration, consistent brand signals, and competitive positioning. Where They Overlap

The overlap is large and important. The single best AEO technique, writing self-contained, extractable answers to specific questions, is also one of the best GEO techniques, because generative engines cite the same kind of clearly structured, extractable content. The citation data confirms it: self-contained chunks of fifty to a hundred and fifty words get cited far more often, which is exactly what good AEO produces. So when you do AEO well, you are also doing a meaningful piece of GEO.

The difference is what AEO leaves out. AEO alone does not address whether AI models trust your brand as an entity, whether independent sources corroborate what you do, or whether you get recommended over competitors. Those are GEO concerns that sit above the question level.

The cleanest way to hold the relationship in your head: AEO is a technique. GEO is a strategy. Strong AEO is one of the building blocks of GEO, but GEO also includes entity authority and brand recommendation that AEO does not touch. You can do AEO without GEO, but you cannot do complete GEO without AEO.

Which Should You Focus On?

For most B2B brands in 2026, the answer is GEO as the strategy, with AEO practices included as a tactic. Here is the reasoning:

  • B2B buying decisions are increasingly influenced inside generative AI responses, not just answer boxes. Being recommended when a buyer asks ChatGPT to compare options matters more than owning a single snippet.
  • GEO subsumes AEO. A complete GEO program already includes the AEO work of structuring extractable answers, so framing your effort around GEO ensures you do the AEO pieces without stopping there.
  • The brand-level factors only GEO addresses are often the bottleneck. Many brands write decent answer content but still do not get recommended, because they lack the entity authority and corroboration that GEO covers and AEO does not. See why B2B SaaS brands stay invisible.

So do not treat it as AEO versus GEO. Adopt GEO as your strategy, and execute AEO techniques, clear question-answer structure, FAQ schema, extractable chunks, as the content-level layer within it. Add entity authority, schema markup, and multi-source corroboration on top, and you have a complete program.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between AEO and GEO?

AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) optimizes content to be the direct answer to a specific question, across snippets, voice, and AI summaries. GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) is broader: optimizing your brand to be cited and recommended within full LLM responses. AEO is question-focused and predates LLMs; GEO is brand-and-entity focused and built for generative AI. They overlap, but GEO additionally covers entity authority and brand recommendation.

Is AEO the same as GEO?

No, though the terms are often used interchangeably. AEO structures content to be extracted as the answer to a question. GEO is broader and brand-centric, requiring entity authority and corroboration in addition to answer-ready content. Strong AEO is one component within a complete GEO strategy.

Should I focus on AEO or GEO for my B2B brand?

Pursue GEO as the strategy, with AEO techniques included as a tactic. GEO is the broader goal of being recommended and cited by AI engines when buyers research your category. AEO techniques like clear Q&A structure and FAQ schema are essential building blocks within that strategy. Do not choose one over the other.

Does AEO still matter now that GEO exists?

Yes. Answer engines have expanded, not disappeared. Snippets, voice assistants, and AI answer summaries still reward content structured as clear, direct answers, and that same structure makes content easy for generative engines to cite. AEO has been absorbed into GEO as a foundational technique, not replaced by it.

The Bottom Line

AEO and GEO are not competitors and not synonyms. AEO is the technique of being the clear answer to a question. GEO is the strategy of being the recommended brand across generative AI. Do AEO well, then build the brand-level entity authority and corroboration that turns good answers into actual recommendations. That combination is what gets you cited.
