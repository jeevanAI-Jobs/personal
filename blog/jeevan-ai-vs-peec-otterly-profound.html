Jeevan AI vs Peec AI vs Otterly vs Profound

An honest head-to-head of the leading AI visibility and GEO tools, on coverage, pricing, and what each one is actually best at.

Disclosure: this comparison is published by Jeevan AI, one of the tools compared. We have tried to represent each competitor fairly and link to sources so you can verify pricing and features yourself.

If you are evaluating tools to track how your brand shows up in ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and Google AI Mode, four names come up most: Profound, Peec AI, Otterly.ai, and Jeevan AI. They are not the same product solving the same problem at different prices. They sit at genuinely different points on the spectrum from raw analytics to done-for-you action.

This guide compares them on the things that actually decide the purchase: engine coverage, what you get, price, and the job each is best suited for. Where a competitor is the better fit, we say so.

Profound: the enterprise analytics platform

Profound is the most analytically deep option. It tracks brand mentions and citations across the major engines, offers conversation-level insight, and is built for large teams and agencies that want a rich data layer. The trade-off is price and complexity: it starts around $99 and climbs to $499 or more, and it is more dashboard than prescription. If your team has analysts who will live in the data and a budget to match, Profound is strong. If you want to be told what to do next, it asks more of you.

Peec AI: solid mid-market tracking

Peec AI is a clean, capable tracker for monitoring share of voice across AI engines, with good reporting for teams. It sits in the mid range around $99 a month. It does the core job well, but like most trackers it is oriented toward measurement: it shows you where you stand more than it hands you a plan to improve. For a mid-market team that mainly needs reliable visibility reporting, it is a reasonable pick.

Otterly.ai: the budget entry point

Otterly.ai is the cheapest credible way to start, with a Lite tier at $29 a month covering a limited set of prompts across a few engines, per Otterly's own pricing page. It is a good fit for a single brand watching a handful of core queries. Watch for add-on costs: some engines cost extra on lower plans, so the headline price is not always the full price.

Jeevan AI: diagnosis plus a content plan

Jeevan AI tracks your brand across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Claude, and Google AI Mode like the others, but its defining difference is what happens after the scan. It scores your gaps on the five buying factors AI uses to recommend brands, then generates a prioritized content plan that tells you what to publish next. It starts with a free scan and paid plans from $49. The philosophy: a score you watch is not the same as a next action you can take. If you want to close the gap, not just measure it, that is the design goal. You can see the full breakdown on the features page.

The honest summary: pick Profound for analytics depth, Peec for mid-market reporting, Otterly for the cheapest tracking, and Jeevan AI when you want the diagnosis and the fix in one place with a free way to start.

How to choose between them

Decide what job you are hiring the tool for. If it is deep analysis for a large team, Profound. If it is dependable reporting in the mid market, Peec. If it is the lowest-cost way to watch a few queries, Otterly. If it is turning visibility into a concrete content plan, Jeevan AI. Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best AI visibility tool for a B2B SaaS brand on a budget?

It depends on what you need. Otterly.ai is the cheapest credible entry around $29 a month for prompt tracking. Profound is the enterprise option starting near $99 and running much higher, with deep analytics. Peec AI sits in the mid range around $99. Jeevan AI offers a free scan and pairs tracking with a content plan, which suits teams that want a next action rather than only a dashboard. Match the tool to whether you need raw analytics, a budget tracker, or diagnosis plus a fix.

What is a good Profound alternative?

Profound is powerful but enterprise priced, so smaller teams often look elsewhere. Lower-cost alternatives include Otterly.ai for simple prompt tracking, Peec AI in the mid range, and Jeevan AI if you want tracking combined with a prioritized content plan and a free way to start. The right alternative depends on whether you mainly need analytics depth or a clear set of actions to improve.

How is Jeevan AI different from Peec AI and Otterly?

Peec AI and Otterly focus on tracking where your brand appears across AI engines and reporting a score. Jeevan AI does that tracking too, but its core difference is that every scan ends with a prioritized content plan that tells you what to publish next, scored on the buying factors AI uses to recommend brands. It is built to produce an action, not only a measurement, and it offers a free scan to start.

There is no single best tool, only the best fit for the job you are hiring it to do. The market splits cleanly: analytics depth (Profound), mid-market reporting (Peec), budget tracking (Otterly), and diagnosis-to-action (Jeevan AI). Be honest about which job is yours, and the choice gets easy.
