ChatGPT Recommending the Wrong Product for Your Brand — Why It Happens and How to Fix It

Jeevan AI  ·  July 4, 2026  ·  8 min read

Getting your brand into AI recommendations is hard. But there is a second problem that gets talked about far less — and causes more damage when it happens.

AI is mentioning you. But it is recommending you for the wrong thing.

ChatGPT is telling buyers you are an enterprise tool when you sell to small teams. Perplexity is placing you in comparison lists with products you do not compete with. Gemini is describing your product as if it solves a problem you solved two product versions ago. And customers arrive confused, pre-framed with the wrong expectations, and often leave frustrated.

Real scenario

"ChatGPT told them the wrong product, what a nightmare. A customer came expecting enterprise analytics. We sell to small teams. Spent 45 minutes on a call and they left frustrated. The AI set up completely the wrong comparison."

This is the AI positioning problem. And it is more common than most marketers realize, because most brands only track whether they appear in AI — not what AI says when it mentions them.

Why this problem is harder to catch than pure invisibility

When a brand is completely invisible in AI search, the problem is obvious. You ask ChatGPT about your category and your name never appears. You know something is wrong.

When a brand is being mispositoned, the problem hides. You show up in searches. Your team sees your name in AI responses. It feels like the visibility work is done. But the customers arriving from those AI mentions are the wrong ones — wrong company size, wrong use case, wrong budget expectations. And because they come in already shaped by an AI-generated description of what you are, they are harder to reframe on a call.

The revenue impact of wrong positioning can actually be worse than zero visibility, because it generates sales activity without generating pipeline.

How AI decides what category to put you in

AI builds its model of your brand from everything it can find: your website content, third-party reviews and directory listings, press mentions, social profiles, and structured data. It looks for consistent patterns across all of those sources to determine:

  • What problem category does this brand belong to?
  • What type of buyer is it best suited for?
  • What comparison set should it appear in?
  • What claims can be attributed to this brand with confidence?

When those signals are clear and consistent, AI positions you accurately. When they are vague, incomplete, or contradictory, AI fills in the gaps with assumptions. And those assumptions are based on pattern-matching against similar-looking brands — which may share surface characteristics with you but serve a completely different market.

The comparison set problem: who AI thinks you compete with

One of the most damaging forms of wrong AI positioning is being placed in the wrong comparison set. In my experience auditing AI recommendations for B2B brands, this is where I see the most hidden revenue damage.

When a buyer asks ChatGPT "what are the alternatives to [your brand]", the answer shapes how they evaluate everything that comes next. If AI names three products you do not actually compete with — products that serve a different buyer size, have a different pricing model, or solve a different problem — the buyer starts comparing you on criteria that are irrelevant to your actual positioning.

They ask about features you do not have because those competitors have them. They balk at your pricing because the comparison products they were shown are priced differently. They arrive at your sales call expecting something that is not what you sell.

The comparison set problem is driven by content structure, not product quality. Brands end up in wrong comparison sets because their positioning overlaps on keywords with categories they do not belong to. Fixing it requires making your positioning so specific that AI cannot reasonably lump you in with the wrong group.

4 root causes of AI brand mispostioning

Generic homepage headlines

"The all-in-one platform for teams" tells AI almost nothing. It is a pattern that matches dozens of categories simultaneously. When AI cannot distinguish what you are from surface-level language, it defaults to the category it most commonly associates with similar-sounding brands. If you look like a project management tool because your language pattern-matches to project management tools, AI may categorize you as one even if you are not.

Inconsistent language across pages

Your homepage says "enterprise-ready". Your pricing page shows plans starting at $19/month. Your case studies feature two-person startups. AI sees contradictory signals and picks one version at random to use as its representation of your brand. The version it picks may not be the one you want buyers to see first.

Missing use-case specificity

If you solve five different problems but describe each of them only vaguely on your website, AI cannot reliably match you to specific buyer queries. It may cite you for the use case that happens to have the most text written about it — not necessarily the one that generates your best customers.

Stale content that no longer reflects your product

AI indexes your entire site, including old blog posts, old case studies, and old landing pages from two product pivots ago. If your website contains content describing a version of your product you no longer sell, AI may still cite that description. Auditing and updating or removing stale positioning content is often part of the AI positioning fix.

How to find out what AI actually thinks about your brand

The fastest way is to ask directly. Open ChatGPT and Perplexity and run these three queries:

  1. "What does [your brand] do?"
  2. "Who is [your brand] best for?"
  3. "What are the alternatives to [your brand]?"

Write down the answers. Compare them to how you would answer those questions. The gaps between what AI says and what you would say are your positioning problems.

Fixing your AI search positioning

There is no shortcut here. The fix is clearer, more specific, more consistent content about who you are and who you serve. Specifically:

  • Rewrite your homepage headline to name the specific buyer type and specific problem you solve
  • Audit every page on your site for contradictory positioning signals and resolve them
  • Build dedicated use case pages for each job your product does, in buyer language
  • Remove or update content that describes an old version of your product
  • Make sure the same positioning language appears consistently across homepage, pricing page, case studies, and about page

Consistency is the key signal. AI builds confidence in a brand representation when it sees the same description repeated across multiple pages and multiple sources. One specific, consistent positioning statement repeated across your entire site is more powerful than five different versions of who you are.

Do not try to appear in every category. Some brands try to broaden their AI positioning by adding content for every possible use case. This creates the exact contradictions that cause AI mispostioning. Narrower, more specific positioning wins more citations for the right queries. Broad, vague positioning wins fewer citations for none of the right queries.

Frequently asked questions

Why is ChatGPT recommending my brand for the wrong use case?

AI builds its model of your brand from patterns across your website and third-party sources. When your positioning is vague, inconsistent, or too broad, AI fills the gaps with assumptions based on similar-looking brands. The fix is clearer, more specific, more consistent content about who you are and who you serve.

What is the AI comparison set problem?

When AI recommends your brand, it often places you alongside other brands it considers comparable. If that comparison set is wrong, buyers arrive pre-framed with the wrong expectations and compare you on irrelevant criteria. Fixing your AI positioning also fixes which comparison set you appear in.

How do I find out what category ChatGPT thinks I am in?

Ask ChatGPT directly: "What does [your brand] do?", "Who is [your brand] best for?", and "What are the alternatives to [your brand]?" The answers reveal how AI has categorized you. Jeevan AI runs this audit automatically across multiple platforms and scores the gaps.
